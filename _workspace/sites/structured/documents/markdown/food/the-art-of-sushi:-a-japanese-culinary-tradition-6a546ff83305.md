# The Art of Sushi: A Japanese Culinary Tradition

Sushi is more than just raw fish; it's an intricate Japanese culinary art form that emphasizes fresh ingredients, precise preparation, and aesthetic presentation. This article delves into the history, types, and etiquette surrounding sushi, offering insights into what makes it a global delicacy.

## Key Components of Sushi

- **Nori**: Dried seaweed used to wrap rolls.
- **Sushi-meshi**: Specially prepared vinegared rice.
- **Neta**: The toppings, typically seafood (raw or cooked), but also vegetables or egg.
- **Wasabi & Gari**: Green horseradish paste and pickled ginger, served as condiments.

Mastering sushi requires years of dedication, but understanding its fundamentals can enhance your appreciation for this exquisite cuisine.

## Types of Sushi

- **Nigiri**: Sliced fish over a small mound of rice.
- **Maki**: Rice and fillings rolled in nori, then sliced.
- **Sashimi**: Sliced raw fish without rice.
- **Temaki**: Hand-rolled cone-shaped sushi.

## Etiquette Tips

- Eat nigiri with your hands.
- Dip fish-side down into soy sauce.
- Eat ginger between different types of sushi to cleanse the palate.
